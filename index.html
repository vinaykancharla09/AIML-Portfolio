The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

Reference Resource · 12 slides

AI/ML History Timeline (1950 – 2026)

Presentation deck · designed as a shareable, at-a-glance field guide

Introduction

A twelve-slide visual timeline of the history of artificial intelligence, organized into seven distinct eras — from the field's philosophical foundations, through both "AI winters," to the deep-learning revolution and today's reasoning and agentic models. It's built to be read quickly and shared: a reference for understanding not just what happened, but why the field moved in cycles.

Description

The deck opens with a color-coded overview band mapping all seven eras at a glance, then devotes individual slides to each period. The two AI winters get dedicated cause-and-impact breakdowns, since the story of AI is as much about its collapses as its breakthroughs. A key-players slide credits the researchers and companies behind each era, and a final slide carries a full reference list and an AI-tool disclosure.

Objective

To turn scattered, hype-heavy AI history into one clear, chronological resource — and in doing so, to demonstrate that I can research a fast-moving technical subject, separate signal from marketing, and communicate it in a form a non-specialist audience can actually use.

Process
  • Researched milestones across primary and secondary sources — IBM, TechTarget, Our World in Data, IEEE, Wikipedia's AI timeline — cross-checking every date before including it.
  • Organized ~40 milestones into seven coherent eras, deciding where each period began and ended and what the through-line was.
  • Designed a high-contrast visual system: a color code distinguishing growth eras, hype peaks, and funding "winters."
  • Built the deck programmatically, then ran visual QA on every slide to catch overlaps and overflow.
  • Added a transparent AI-tool disclosure and a reference list separating directly-read sources from search-found ones.

Value Proposition

This artifact shows I can take an ambiguous, sprawling topic and produce a trustworthy, well-sourced deliverable that teaches. In a field crowded with confident but shallow AI explainers, careful research and honest sourcing is the value.

Unique Value

Most AI timelines celebrate the wins. This one gives equal weight to the two AI winters and treats them as the interesting part — because understanding why AI has failed before is exactly what a healthcare organization needs from someone advising on adoption today. That framing reflects how I think about technology risk.

Relevance

For my audience — healthcare-technology and data teams weighing AI adoption — historical context is decision-support. Knowing the pattern of hype, overreach, and correction helps a team set realistic expectations before investing.
